# Welcome

Welcome to Notable Events, a log of various events/news within the Wizard101 and KI community, with the purpose of making events easy to refer to in the future. In the next paragraph, you can learn how to get the most out of this page.

Okay so, how do I find stuff here?

Good question! You might be looking at the flurry of information and wondering how to navigate it. There are a few ways to navigate this page, highlighted below.

  • You can look at the Monthly Overview found at the beginning of every Month.
  • You can use your browser search function or the Search bar above to search for keywords or tags.
  • We'll also create and tag specific pages for important events and eventually have a filter.
  • We are working on the navigability (new word) of this resource!

Generic:

⭐ The Generic tags denote "re-occuring" events or known people. They are in blue.

  • KingsIsle will be indicated with: [KI]
  • Pirate101 will be indicated with: [P101]
  • W101Devs will be indicated with: [WD] or [WD/Important]
  • Information about a New World is indicated with: [NewWorld]
  • KingsIsle Live will be indicated with: [KILive]
  • KI leave/join will be indicated with: [KIEmployees]
  • Controversy will be indicated with: [Controversy] or [Controversy+] if major.
  • Bugs will be indicated with: [Bugs], [MajorBugs], [PartOffline] or [Offline] [1]
  • Roundtable event will be indifcated with: [Roundtable]
  • Loyalty Store refresh will be indicated with: [LoyaltyStoreRefresh]

  • Wizard101's Birthday will be indicated with: [BDAY]

3. KingsIsle Live - September 2023

KingsIsle Live (September 28th, 2023)

Gamescom & PAX West

- Gamescom was a **little talking to press and a lot of talking business.** They talked with at least 50 different groups, and so picked up tons of potential partners.

  • Then they flew out to PAX, a little bit of business, and a lot of press and community. They introduced Wizard101 and Pirate101 to the people there.

  • They had many comments about the game's influence on people's childhood. In fact, one game developer from another booth came over and said “They’re in games because of their game.”

State of the Games

Wizard101

(NA) They’ve dropped Wallaru teasers on the micro-site most Fridays. They’ll have more to talk about next month, potentially after an upcoming update.

(EU) Working on payment options, they’ve hit a major snag that’s not within their control. Working to resolve the issues, and will keep you posted.

(EU) Steam. They’re working to make it a reality, but it’s taking time due to backend complications.

(EU) Fall update coming, which should bring EU up to NA’s equivalent of April 2022. They are still on their quest to catch up, and have made great progress since acquiring EU.

Pirate101

• Sinbad Part 3 represents a milestone. They didn't commit to the story, and it was left in a spot where it could be the conclusion. The Sinbad Chapters bean as an experiment, to see what they could create, and to get people working on Pirate.

• Sinbad was going to be a side project, but things accelerated (especially w/Steam). They decided to stop waiting on story content, but they'll have episodes instead of books. In Sinbad Chapter 3, they wrap the story up, mention that the story is beginning, and "El Dorado".

Section 1 - Wizard101 North America

  • Microsite: More phases are planned for the website. This is Phase 1, with phases planned for test realm and live launch, and will keep expanding the concept for future updates.

  • P101 on new Macs: Partners are working on fixes for some issues, so they'll keep you posted.

  • In terms of lore books - they did float around the idea of actual books. However, they are aware that a common issue with returning players is being unfamiliar with the story. So, it would be nice to have an "in-game means" of where you are, what's going on, and what the story is.

  • “Revelation” is one word to describe Wallaru. You’re going to learn lots of stuff, and lots of questions will be answered. Sandiago will be a big part of Wallaru, and his mysteries will be uncovered by the end of Wallaru.

A clarification from the writers

Wallaru is based on Australia, and Cool Ranch is based on the Old West. They both take place in a “semi-arid” environment and "are at the back half of the 19th Century", so there will be similarities. But, they are not linked. It shares no places, no NPCs, and it has its own plot.

This is addressed as there have been complaints of reuse in the past, but the Outback area is being built completely from scratch. There's a good reason why we're going Wallaru.

Karl and Sam will create a presentation where they talk about the art in Wallaru, inspirations, sources, what they were aiming for, concepts, etc. It’ll happen before they go to test realm.


  • Hope to update gardening one day, but no immediate plans. Thoughts on what you want to see changed can still be considered.

  • Bringing back longer dungeons like The Labyrinth? Plans are being discussed. Just wait.

Small pieces of information

  1. Judgement is voiced by Falmea.
  2. The Eye of History of the Bartleby Plushie can be removed, to allow players to match their progression.
  3. There's a hat for P101's Anniversary that they're excited about + they plan to do a pass on the companions.
  4. Heroes of Lemuria Pack coming to EU (Nov), Pet Trainers Bundle scheduled for October 11th*

*It didn't make the translation pass, so they had to hold it back. They now have the translations

Name change section ft Calamity:

Question: How did you come up with new names?

Most new names were taken from a forum post a few years back. That had like 10 pages of names! There was a lot to consider with balancing demographic names, both with what they had and wanted to add.

They wanted to pull from many cultures, as well as adding names of varying masculinity/femininity or gender neutral, all while keeping the fantasy style they have, and satisfying both categories of people, those who want to align their character to their real identity, or those who want to have a roleplay/fantasy character identity.

Last name was a lot of fantasy words that were pulled from pop culture references, trying to think of the references people could make, and was amazed at the great combos she saw when the feature hit live - some she hadn't even thought of.

Fun fact: Calamity was responsible for finding a lot of fun names, that perhaps shouldn't have been available.

Question: How long did name change take to happen?

Can't speak for programming, but on the design side, it was a multi-stage process. They essentially doubled the amount of names in-game, some from the names suggestions forum post, the rest had to be pulled from other places. It took a while to have the list, and then they had to review it.

They had some challenges implementing names as a tool to add names hadn't been used ever, as all the names came with the original release, and none had been added since. So, it took a bit of time to fix that tech. The biggest challenge is that there is a hard limit of 250 names in each slot.

A slot being first female/first male, middle, and last. That was a hard limit, and while there's plenty of names out there they'd love to accommodate, that's the limit. They also don't think it would be a good player experience to scroll through thousands of names for one.

It was a joy to wake up for the first day of test and see "Olive Garden" on Reddit. Should've seen it coming but didn't, and they spent all day laughing at the hilarious name combinations in their Team Discord!

    What's a build, and how does without a build work?

    • When they put new releases of the game, they have to do a build , this build can take a couple of hours to do, then it needs to be QA'd, approved, and servers need to be pulled down (normally on Wednesday at 2am CT), load the new build up, update the patcher, and the patcher distributes the files to players, and then the players are on the new build.

    • When this "without a build" system was added, it means that certain things can be updated in-game without a full build , so if there are emergency things, they can go in and fix them without server downtime & hours/days of effort. In pirate, this will give them a quicker turnaround time.

    > Novus Roundtable (Summary) - December 2nd, 2022 (ONE OF TWO)
> 
> - Originally, the [4th] Arc was going to be three worlds, but that changed as development progressed. The theme was also different to previous arcs. They have seen a few comments about a lack of a “big bad” but they are experimenting.
> 
> - Interesting thing about Novus is that it’s a new world in the Spiral that changes the longer you are in contact with it. That “notion” was the first idea they had for Arc 4. The introduction of the new world actually almost happened at the end of Empyrea II, but the more they thought about it, the more they realised that it was “too much too fast”.
> 
> - Wanted the player to get time to know “The Nothing” and put it into context, so they “let it breathe” a lot more. In previous worlds, you deal with The Nothing (i.e Dasein) - giving them an opportunity to tell an interesting story about “existential philosophy”, what it means to exist, be good, etc.
> 
> - Lack of “the big bad” was to change the pace and start slow. Think of Karamelle as a Prologue instead of the 1st World of the 4th Arc. You meet “The Nothing”, but it is very brief and with so little context that it’s confusing. Then in Lemuria, they explain what happened and build on from there.
> 
> - Given Dell’s passing, it would be disrespectful to her to even sound alike the character, so they feel like the game has grown beyond Raven - and they’ve tried to set things up so that a Narrator is not required.
> 
> - Intent at the end of Empyrea was to take Raven & Spider off the stage, if they remained and had opinions about everything, they could fix it. What are the players here for if they can fix everything? They have moved on. As for Bat and Mellori, that is a different question. No plans but the opportunity is there.
> 
> Q: What world would’ve conquered Novus if the Wiz wasn’t involved.
> - Karl: Marleybone, because they’re a big colonial power, that’s what they do. Maybe Valencia, because they’ve got the tech and money, and with their Clockworks, it could be interesting. Valencia and Marleybone would really fight down to the wire. The Penguins aren’t organised, and the Monquistans are outnumbered. It would come down to the Unicorns or the Dogs. 
> 
> Kyle: Have to agree with Marleybone. They got there first and got a lay off the land. Polaris and Monquista had an alliance, but if the player didn’t get involved, they might’ve not broken up.
> 
> - They’ve been eager to throw some P101 into Wizard101 for some time. The way they look at it, they have a “spiral cinematic universe”. They did a lot of Wizard101 callouts in pirate, but not often the other way. 
> 
> - As for the lack of Armada until this point, Wizard for the longest time wasn’t much into giving shoutouts to Pirate. About the time the Wizard got to Polaris, things got weird in the timeline. For example, where is Napoleguin? They stopped trying to marry the timelines together at this point, as the Polaris story was going in a direction that Napoleguin would’ve disrupted. However, the original intention was that the game’s timelines were roughly concurrent. 
> 
> - Next world (Wallaru) will wrap up Arc 4. The problem in the next world will be related to the Spiral Powers gunning for the Wizard, and they ultimately wanted to set up a situation where from the point of view of the Spiral, you are the villain that has bought this “thing” into our universe, that is now going to destroy it, and you must be stopped.
>  
> - The art side took reuse complaints to heart with Lemuria, so when they did Novus, they wanted to do it better. A lot of these characters from these worlds P101 needed a touching-up, such as talk animations. For example, they pretty much redid the dogs. They wanted to make sure they gave players what they were asking for - not a whole lot of reuse.
>
> Novus Roundtable (Summary) -  (TWO OF TWO)
> 
> - In Karamelle, you’re dealing with Dasein as an infant. It is only doing what it has seen and is incapable of speech. In Lemuria, Dasein is a child, learning what is good and bad, black and white. In Novus, Dasein hits adolescence - life isn’t fair. What you think of good, may not be good to others. What is good? What if there’s no such thing as good?
> 
> - Dasein has gone through this long quest to be “something”, but when he gets there, it’s “not what he wanted to be”. 
> 
> - The Novus loading screen has pieces from every single zone that hint into the Old One, Dasein, it’s really ‘esoteric’ and they wanted to do the whole “surrealism” thing. Little hints to “whet the appetite”, the spoiler wall was a teaser, and they work with writing to make sure they don’t give too much.  
> 
> Q: Favorite looking area in Novus:

> 
> Sam: Crazy Stairs (hands down). The whole notion of coming up with something that sounds difficult, crazy stairs was that way, no clue how they were going to do it but the end result was better than imagined.
> 
> - New Vicorgia was the first zone where they knew what they wanted to do (i.e Its the Marleybonians they mark their territory). The very first idea for it was that it was going to be this Giant Wagon that had these hydrants on it, and as the wagon moved, it would plant flags as it moved by, and that was the early iteration of what they thought New Vicorgia was going to be. 
> 
> Q: What do you love the most about Novus?
> Sam: Loves how the world jumps between silly and serious (Sam). There are characters that are so self-absorbed or hostile/silly that it’s ridiculous, yet when you look at what they’re doing and the consequences of what they’re doing it’s not at all funny.
> 
> Karl: Loves how much the players love this world. Everything about it is based on Art, and Art is very personal, and when they create Art for Wiz, it’s very personal. Seeing that reception from players is validating down to their core. It’s a rare experience that you get to see people truly appreciate things you’ve done in your lifetime.
> 
> - As for Mustaches, the “big baddie” of that Area has a giant moustache. He is very self-absorbed, and the moustaches are kind of an extension of that, his ego come to life.  That’s why the Moustaches are there.
> 
> - As for other worlds with the shards, either they couldn’t reach them or decided not to. For the list of worlds they included, it was to show that the shards were everywhere more than it was to do with which worlds were named. 
> 
> - Star Gator? This is an ongoing Plot Thread, but they didn’t say more than “It would be telling.”
>

There are body parts - odd body parts, all around Novus, representing that Dasein is the body of the World. Dasein is trying to reverse sadness, so the crying eye was a good image for that. As for the hands, they drive the point home that he has the world in his hands. Things that subtly show that Dasein is the World.
